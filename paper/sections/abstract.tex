\begin{abstract}
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities on
text-to-SQL benchmarks such as Spider and BIRD. However, these benchmarks
evaluate \emph{query generation}, not \emph{structural understanding} of
database schemas. We introduce \textbf{DW-Bench}, the first benchmark
specifically designed to evaluate LLMs on \emph{graph topology reasoning}
over data warehouse schemas. DW-Bench contains \textbf{1,038 questions}
across \textbf{5 datasets} (4 real-world: AdventureWorks, TPC-DS, TPC-DI,
OMOP~CDM; 1 synthetic: Syn-Logistics) spanning \textbf{262 tables},
\textbf{13 question subtypes}, and \textbf{3 difficulty levels}. Questions
require reasoning about foreign key paths, data lineage chains, transitive
impact analysis, and connected component detection---tasks that demand
graph traversal rather than SQL generation.

We evaluate four baselines---\emph{Oracle}, \emph{Flat Text},
\emph{Vector-RAG}, and \emph{Graph-Augmented}---using two frontier models:
Gemini~2.5 Flash~\cite{gemini2024} and DeepSeek-V3~\cite{deepseek2024v3}.
On the original 4 real-world datasets, Gemini micro-averaging creates a
\textbf{Triviality Illusion}: Flat Text appears strongest (73.9\% EM), but
under macro-averaging Graph-Augmented leads (\textbf{68.0\%} vs.\ 61.1\%).
DeepSeek-V3 shows Graph-Augmented \emph{strictly dominating} across both
micro (\textbf{82.7\%}) and macro (\textbf{74.0\%}). On Syn-Logistics,
which was designed to stress-test structural reasoning with $n\geq20$ per
subtype, we observe the most devastating finding:
\texttt{hop\_count} ($n{=}44$) and \texttt{count} ($n{=}26$) score
\textbf{0\% EM across all baselines} while the Oracle achieves 100\%,
proving the structural reasoning gap is fundamental. The Oracle
baseline achieves \textbf{97.8--100\% EM}, confirming question solvability.

DW-Bench includes an obfuscation protocol replacing table names with
random identifiers, revealing that DeepSeek suffers up to \textbf{30\%}
penalty while Gemini shows differential effects across difficulty levels.
The benchmark, all results, and evaluation code are publicly available at
\url{https://github.com/AJamal27891/dw-bench}.
\end{abstract}
