\begin{abstract}
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities on
text-to-SQL benchmarks such as Spider and BIRD. However, these benchmarks
evaluate \emph{query generation}, not \emph{structural understanding} of
database schemas. We introduce \textbf{DW-Bench}, the first benchmark
designed to evaluate LLMs on \emph{graph topology reasoning} over data
warehouse schemas, organized in two tiers: \textbf{Tier~1}
(schema-level, 1,046~questions across 13~subtypes) tests structural
reasoning over foreign key paths, lineage chains, and component
detection; \textbf{Tier~2} (value-level, 433~questions across
8~subtypes) tests row-level provenance tracing and forward impact
analysis. DW-Bench spans \textbf{5~datasets} (4~real-world, 1~synthetic)
with \textbf{262~tables} and \textbf{3~difficulty levels}.

We evaluate six baselines spanning the text-to-agentic spectrum:
Flat~Text, Vector-RAG, Graph-Augmented, Tool-Use, ReAct-Code, and
Oracle, using two frontier models: Gemini~2.5 Flash and DeepSeek-V3.
\textbf{Tool-Use dominates} Tier~1 with 91\% micro-EM, outperforming
all static baselines (69--81\%) by 10--22 percentage points. However,
a \textbf{hard-task ceiling persists}: even Tool-Use plateaus at
$\sim$60\% on hard questions while Oracle achieves 99\%, with
\texttt{combined\_impact} at only 12\% EM. Tier~2 reveals that tools
are \textbf{literally required} for value-level questions
(\texttt{cascade\_count}: 100\% vs.\ 0\% without tools) yet
\textbf{insufficient} overall (59\% EM), with multi-hop tracing (27\%)
and shared-source detection (23\%) remaining unsolved---precisely the
structural reasoning gap that learned graph representations (GNNs)
are designed to fill.

The benchmark, all results, and evaluation code are publicly available at
\url{https://github.com/AJamal27891/dw-bench}.
\end{abstract}
