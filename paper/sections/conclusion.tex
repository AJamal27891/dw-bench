\section{Conclusion}

We introduced DW-Bench, the first benchmark for evaluating LLM
capabilities on data warehouse graph topology reasoning. Across five
datasets (four real-world, one synthetic), two frontier models (Gemini~2.5
Flash and DeepSeek-V3), and five baselines (Flat Text, Graph-Augmented,
Vector-RAG, Tool-Use, and Oracle), our evaluation reveals three key
insights. First, a \emph{model-dependent structural gap}: Gemini achieves
near-perfect scores on structural subtypes (98--100\% EM on
\texttt{hop\_count} and \texttt{count}), while DeepSeek scores as low as
27--48\% on the same tasks. Second, a \emph{Triviality Illusion}: micro-averaging
inflates scores by weighting easy subtypes disproportionately, which
macro-averaging corrects. Third, a \emph{compositional ceiling}:
\texttt{combined\_impact}---requiring multi-phase lineage + FK
traversal---remains below 27\% EM for both models across all baselines,
despite 100\% Oracle solvability, establishing a compelling frontier for
learned graph representations.

DW-Bench is publicly available at
\url{https://github.com/AJamal27891/dw-bench} with all datasets,
evaluation code, and baseline implementations.
