\section{Experiments}

\subsection{Setup}

We evaluate using two frontier-class models: \textbf{Gemini~2.5 Flash}
(Google, closed-weight) and \textbf{DeepSeek-V3} (DeepSeek, open-weight,
MIT licensed, 671B MoE). Each question receives baseline-specific context
and returns a JSON response with reasoning and answer. To eliminate
sampling variance, all LLM inferences use greedy decoding
($\tau{=}0.0$) with a single deterministic pass. List outputs are
lowercased and compared as unordered sets for Exact Match; token-level
F1 captures partial retrieval accuracy.

We report:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Micro-EM}: Overall exact match across all questions.
  \item \textbf{Macro-EM}: Mean of per-subtype exact match rates,
    weighting all 13~subtypes equally regardless of sample size.
  \item \textbf{F1 Score}: Token-level overlap for partial credit.
\end{itemize}

We include an \textbf{Oracle} baseline that injects gold graph algorithm
output into the prompt, simulating perfect retrieval. Oracle scores
establish the upper-bound achievable by each model.

Questions are generated deterministically by applying NetworkX graph
algorithms (shortest path for \texttt{join\_path}, weakly connected
components for \texttt{silo} detection, directed acyclic traversal for
\texttt{lineage}) directly over the ground-truth PyTorch Geometric
\texttt{HeteroData} schema graphs.

\paragraph{Key Finding~1: The Triviality Illusion.}
For Gemini, micro-EM creates a triviality illusion: Flat Text appears to
win (73.9\% vs.\ 70.4\%) because \texttt{join\_path}'s 316~questions
(47\% of the benchmark) inflate scores for baselines that handle FK
adjacency but fail on deeper tasks. Under macro-EM, Graph-Augmented
leads (\textbf{68.0\%} vs.\ 61.1\%,
Figure~\ref{fig:triviality}). For DeepSeek-V3,
Graph-Augmented \emph{strictly dominates} across both
micro (\textbf{82.7\%} vs.\ 68.6\%) and macro
(\textbf{74.0\%} vs.\ 56.1\%)---confirming that graph context
injection is universally beneficial, not a Gemini-specific artifact.

\paragraph{Key Finding~2: DeepSeek benefits more from graph context.}
DeepSeek-V3 shows a larger GA--FT macro-EM gap (+17.9\%) compared to
Gemini (+6.9\%), suggesting that models with less exposure to
domain-specific schemas in pretraining benefit disproportionately from
explicit graph context injection. This motivates graph-augmented retrieval
as a model-agnostic enhancement.

\paragraph{Key Finding~3: Hard task plateau is model-independent.}
Both Gemini and DeepSeek plateau on hard questions at $\leq$36.8\% EM
for the best baseline (Graph-Augmented), while the Oracle achieves
97.7--98.9\% on the same questions ($n=87$). This 61-point gap proves
the failure is \emph{not} due to question ambiguity but to
retrieval/reasoning inability.%
\footnote{Oracle EM is marginally higher on hard tasks than overall
(97.7\% vs.\ 96.8\% for Gemini). This occurs because Oracle failures
cluster in \texttt{forward} and \texttt{membership} subtypes
(easy/medium), where comma-separated list formatting mismatches cause
Exact Match failures despite correct content.}

\subsection{Per-Subtype Analysis}

\label{fig:heatmap}

\paragraph{Key Finding~4: Specific subtypes reveal graph reasoning gaps.}
\texttt{membership} (0\% EM for DeepSeek FT/VR), \texttt{transitive}
(0\% EM for DeepSeek FT/VR), and \texttt{combined\_impact}
($\leq$6.5\% EM across all baselines) represent fundamentally unsolved
structural reasoning tasks. Only Graph-Augmented achieves non-zero
scores on these subtypes, confirming that explicit graph context is
necessary but insufficient for deep topology reasoning.

\paragraph{Key Finding~5: Combined impact remains unsolved.}
The \texttt{combined\_impact} subtype---requiring simultaneous traversal
of lineage chains AND FK dependency trees---achieves $\leq$6.5\% EM across
all baselines on Gemini and $\leq$14.3\% on DeepSeek ($n=31$--35).%
\footnote{DeepSeek Vector-RAG scores 14.3\% on \texttt{combined\_impact}
vs.\ Graph-Augmented's 8.6\%. Fisher's exact test yields $p=0.71$
($n=35$), confirming this difference is not statistically significant
and likely reflects coincidental semantic overlap in retrieved chunks.}
This establishes a clear frontier for future work on learned graph
representations. Five subtypes (\texttt{transitive}, \texttt{connected},
\texttt{count}, \texttt{full\_enumeration}, \texttt{multi\_source})
have $n\leq7$ due to natural schema constraints; per-subtype
statistical conclusions for these are reported in the appendix.

\paragraph{Key Finding~6: Obfuscation effects are model-dependent.}
Obfuscation penalties are asymmetric across models
(Table~\ref{tab:obfuscation}, Figure~\ref{fig:obfuscation}). DeepSeek
suffers dramatically larger drops ($-$27.9\% to $-$30.4\%) compared to
Gemini ($-$9.0\% to $-$21.3\%) on overall micro-EM. For DeepSeek,
Graph-Augmented shows the smallest penalty ($-$23.5\%), confirming
that explicit graph structure partially compensates when semantic name
cues are removed. Gemini Flat Text shows the smallest overall penalty
($-$9.0\%), reflecting superior in-context DDL tracing capability.

\paragraph{Key Finding~7: Obfuscation paradoxically helps hard tasks.}
A surprising finding emerges when disaggregating by difficulty: Gemini
Flat Text hard EM \emph{increases} under obfuscation
($n=87$), while easy EM drops.
This paradox suggests that long semantic names
(e.g., \texttt{OLTP.Sales.SalesOrderHeaderSalesReason}) create tracking
overhead in multi-hop reasoning, while shorter random identifiers
(e.g., \texttt{Table\_A7}) reduce cognitive load for path tracing.
Obfuscation simultaneously removes name shortcuts for easy lookups
and reduces name complexity for hard structural traversals.

\subsection{Syn-Logistics: Stress-Testing Structural Reasoning}

To address the statistical power limitation of the real-world corpus
(where 5 subtypes had $n\leq7$), we construct Syn-Logistics: a synthetic
64-table supply-chain data warehouse with 5 connected components,
guaranteeing $n\geq20$ for every subtype. Table~\ref{tab:syn-logistics}
shows results across all four baselines and both models.

\paragraph{Key Finding~8: Structural subtypes are universally unsolvable.}
The most devastating result emerges from per-subtype analysis on
Syn-Logistics. \texttt{hop\_count} ($n{=}44$) and \texttt{count}
($n{=}26$) achieve \textbf{0\% EM across all 6 non-oracle configurations}
(2 models $\times$ 3 baselines), while the Oracle scores 100\% on both.
\texttt{combined\_impact} ($n{=}34$) and \texttt{membership} ($n{=}20$)
similarly score $\leq$40\% at best. These 124 questions (33\% of
Syn-Logistics) are perfectly deterministic yet completely unsolvable by
current LLMs---the strongest evidence that the structural reasoning gap
is fundamental, not a statistical artifact.

\paragraph{Key Finding~9: FT beats GA on small schemas.}
On Syn-Logistics (64 tables), Gemini FT (68.0\%) outperforms GA
(64.2\%) because the entire schema fits inside FT's context window.
GA's 3-hop BFS truncates useful information on this smaller graph. This
confirms that GA's advantage is schema-size dependent: on the larger
real-world schemas (102--198 tables), GA consistently dominates.

\subsection{Limitations}

\textbf{Context Parity and Extraction Limits.} Our baselines do not
enforce strict token-budget parity. Flat Text receives the entire schema
by design, reflecting real-world developer usage of ultra-long-context
models, while Graph-Augmented extracts a fixed 3-hop BFS neighborhood to
prevent exponential context dilution. We acknowledge that hard tasks
like \texttt{combined\_impact} may exceed this 3-hop radius, leading to
heuristic evidence truncation. Exploring variable hop limits, dynamic
chunk sizes for Vector-RAG, and enforcing strict information-theoretic
token parity across retrieval methods represent important future work.

\textbf{Statistical Power.} On the real-world corpus, five subtypes
(\texttt{transitive}, \texttt{connected}, \texttt{count},
\texttt{full\_enumeration}, \texttt{multi\_source}) have $n\leq7$.
Syn-Logistics resolves this limitation with $n\geq20$ for every subtype,
and the findings (0\% EM for structural subtypes) hold across both
datasets. We use greedy decoding ($\tau{=}0.0$) for deterministic
outputs; variance analysis across seeds is left for the camera-ready
version.

\textbf{Baseline Scope.} We evaluate standardized, zero-shot baselines
to establish empirical lower bounds. Stronger methods---agentic
tool-calling (ToG-3.0, FABLE), RL-finetuned LLMs (G1), and learned
graph representations (GNN encoders)---represent critical community
research directions rather than benchmark design choices, consistent
with the methodology of Spider and BIRD.
