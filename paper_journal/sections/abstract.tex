\begin{abstract}
\noindent Graph topology reasoning over data warehouse schemas — understanding
foreign key paths, lineage chains, connected components, and multi-hop impact
propagation — is a capability that frontier LLMs demonstrably lack, yet one
that data engineers rely on daily.  We introduce \textbf{DW-Bench}, the first
benchmark specifically designed to measure this capability across the full
spectrum of context injection strategies.

DW-Bench is organized in two tiers.  \textbf{Tier~1} (schema-level,
1,046~questions across 13~subtypes and 3~difficulty levels) tests structural
reasoning over foreign key paths, lineage chains, and connected component
detection using five datasets (four real-world: AdventureWorks, TPC-DS,
TPC-DI, OMOP~CDM) and one synthetic (Syn-Logistics) encompassing
262~tables and 521~edges.  \textbf{Tier~2} (value-level, 433~questions
across 8~subtypes) extends evaluation to row-level provenance tracing and
forward impact analysis, requiring interactive data access beyond any
context window.

We evaluate six baselines spanning the text-only to agentic spectrum
with three frontier LLMs (Gemini~2.5 Flash, DeepSeek-V3, Qwen2.5-72B).
Four findings emerge: (1)~Tool-Use dominates schema-level reasoning
with 89--90\% micro-EM versus 69--77\% for static baselines; (2)~static
baselines exhibit a ``triviality illusion'' where easy subtypes inflate
aggregate scores; (3)~even tool-augmented LLMs plateau at $\sim$60\% on
hard compositional reasoning (versus $>$99\% Oracle), pointing to a
fundamental representational bottleneck; and (4)~on Tier~2 value-level
questions, tools are \emph{necessary} (up to 100\% vs.\ 0\% on
data-dependent subtypes) but \emph{insufficient} for multi-hop structural
reasoning (27\% on \texttt{multi\_hop\_trace}).
An obfuscation protocol confirms that Tool-Use operates algorithmically
($\Delta{\leq}{-}4$\%) while static baselines lose 9--32\% EM under name
randomization, distinguishing genuine topology understanding from schema
name memorization.

All datasets, evaluation code, and baseline implementations are publicly
released to support evaluation of future graph reasoning systems, hybrid
retrieval architectures, and GNN-augmented LLM approaches on this class of
structural tasks.
\end{abstract}
